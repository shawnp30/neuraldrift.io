**SD 1.5 vs SDXL vs LCM vs Turbo vs Flux - Which should you use?**

---

## Overview

Different Stable Diffusion architectures exist for different purposes. Using the wrong type causes errors, poor quality, or incompatibility.

---

## 🎨 SD 1.5 (Stable Diffusion 1.5)

### Best For
- Fast generation
- Anime and stylized art
- Low VRAM GPUs (4-8GB)
- Beginners
- Experimentation

### Specifications
- **Resolution:** 512×512 native (can upscale)
- **VRAM:** 4GB minimum
- **Speed:** Fast (10-20 sec per image on RTX 3060)
- **File size:** 2-4 GB

### When to Use
- ✅ Learning ComfyUI
- ✅ Quick iterations
- ✅ Anime/cartoon styles
- ✅ Low-end hardware
- ✅ Large batch generation

### When NOT to Use
- ❌ Photorealistic commercial work
- ❌ High-resolution output without upscaling
- ❌ Fine detail requirements

---

## 🎨 SDXL (Stable Diffusion XL)

### Best For
- Photorealism
- Commercial quality
- High-detail images
- Professional work

### Specifications
- **Resolution:** 1024×1024 native
- **VRAM:** 8GB minimum, 12GB+ recommended
- **Speed:** Slower (30-60 sec per image on RTX 3060)
- **File size:** 6-7 GB

### When to Use
- ✅ Photorealistic images
- ✅ Professional quality needed
- ✅ High-resolution requirements
- ✅ Commercial projects
- ✅ Better text rendering

### When NOT to Use
- ❌ Low VRAM (< 8GB)
- ❌ Need fast iteration
- ❌ Anime/stylized preferred
- ❌ Limited disk space

---

## ⚡ LCM (Latent Consistency Models)

### Best For
- Ultra-fast generation
- Real-time previews
- Rapid prototyping

### Specifications
- **Steps:** 2-8 (vs 20-50 normal)
- **Speed:** 2-5 seconds per image
- **Quality:** Lower than SD 1.5/SDXL

### When to Use
- ✅ Concept exploration
- ✅ Quick drafts
- ✅ Real-time generation
- ✅ Testing compositions

### When NOT to Use
- ❌ Final quality images
- ❌ Fine details needed
- ❌ Professional work

---

## 🚀 Turbo Models

### Best For
- 1-step generation
- Maximum speed
- Experimentation

### Specifications
- **Steps:** 1-4
- **Speed:** 1-3 seconds per image
- **Quality:** Lowest, but usable

### When to Use
- ✅ Instant feedback
- ✅ Composition testing
- ✅ Live generation demos

### When NOT to Use
- ❌ Any final output
- ❌ Client work
- ❌ Detailed images

---

## 🌟 Flux

### Best For
- Cutting-edge quality
- Best prompt following
- State-of-art results

### Specifications
- **Resolution:** 1024×1024+
- **VRAM:** 16GB minimum, 24GB+ recommended
- **Speed:** Very slow (60-120 sec)
- **File size:** 12-24 GB

### When to Use
- ✅ Maximum quality needed
- ✅ Best prompt adherence
- ✅ High-end hardware available
- ✅ Complex compositions

### When NOT to Use
- ❌ Limited VRAM
- ❌ Need fast iteration
- ❌ Limited disk space
- ❌ Custom nodes (compatibility still growing)

## 🔧 Compatibility Guide

### LoRAs
- SD 1.5 LoRA → SD 1.5 checkpoint ONLY
- SDXL LoRA → SDXL checkpoint ONLY
- Flux LoRA → Flux checkpoint ONLY

**Mixing = Errors or crashes**

---

### ControlNet
- SD 1.5 ControlNet → SD 1.5 models
- SDXL ControlNet → SDXL models

---

### VAE
- Most SD 1.5 models: Need external VAE
- SDXL: Usually has built-in VAE
- Flux: Custom VAE handling

## 🎯 Recommended Starting Point

### For Beginners
**SD 1.5** - Learn workflow basics fast

**Recommended checkpoint:**
- `realisticVision_v51.safetensors` (realism)
- `anything-v5.safetensors` (anime)

---

### For Intermediate Users
**SDXL** - Professional quality

**Recommended checkpoint:**
- `sd_xl_base_1.0.safetensors`

---

### For Advanced Users
**Flux** - Cutting edge

**Requirement:**
- RTX 4080 or better
- 24GB+ VRAM

---

## 🆘 Model Loading Errors

### "Model failed to load"
**Cause:** Wrong model type for workflow

**Fix:** Check workflow requirements, use matching model type

---

### "Unexpected key in state dict"
**Cause:** Model architecture mismatch

**Fix:** Verify exact model version needed (SD1.5 vs SDXL vs Flux)
